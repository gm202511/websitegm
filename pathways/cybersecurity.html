🛡️ Cybersecurity Pathway

Learn the core concepts of cybersecurity, how attackers operate, and the steps professionals take to defend systems and respond to incidents.

Cybersecurity Foundations

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks, accounts, and data from unauthorized access or damage. Cybersecurity professionals focus on keeping information confidential, accurate, and available when needed.

Threats & Social Engineering

Many attacks focus on people rather than technology. Social engineering tricks users into giving away passwords, clicking dangerous links, or sharing information. Phishing emails, fake websites, and suspicious text messages are common examples.

Defense in Depth & Security Controls

Defense in depth means using multiple layers of protection instead of relying on only one tool. Firewalls, strong passwords, multi-factor authentication, antivirus software, and security policies work together to reduce risk.

Incident Response

Incident response is the process of reacting to a security event. Security teams identify the problem, contain it, remove the threat, recover systems, and learn from the incident to prevent it from happening again.

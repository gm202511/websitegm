⚠️ Cybersecurity Threats

Cybersecurity threats come in many forms. Some target systems and networks directly, while others rely on malicious software or human behavior.

What Is a Cybersecurity Threat?

A cybersecurity threat is anything that has the potential to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to computers, networks, or data. Threats may be intentional (attacks) or unintentional (misconfigurations or user error), but in this lesson we focus on **deliberate threats**.

Malware Threats

Malware is short for malicious software. These programs are designed to harm systems, steal data, or give attackers control over devices.

  • Viruses – Attach to legitimate files and spread when users run infected programs.
  • Worms – Spread automatically across networks without user interaction.
  • Trojans – Disguised as legitimate software but perform malicious actions.
  • Ransomware – Encrypts files and demands payment to restore access.
  • Spyware – Secretly monitors user activity and collects information.

Malware often enters systems through phishing emails, malicious downloads, or unpatched vulnerabilities.

Network-Based Threats

Network threats focus on disrupting, intercepting, or abusing network communication. These attacks usually target availability, confidentiality, or integrity of data.

  • Denial-of-Service (DoS) – Overwhelms systems with traffic to make them unavailable.
  • Distributed DoS (DDoS) – Multiple systems attack a target at once.
  • Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) – Intercepts communication between two parties.
  • Packet sniffing – Captures unencrypted network traffic.
  • Unauthorized access – Exploiting weak credentials or open ports.

Strong passwords, encryption, and firewalls help reduce network-based threats.

Malicious Actors

Threats don’t happen on their own — they are carried out by people or groups called malicious actors. These actors have different motivations and skill levels.

  • Script kiddies – Use existing tools without fully understanding them.
  • Cybercriminals – Motivated by financial gain.
  • Insiders – Employees or students abusing authorized access.
  • Hacktivists – Attack systems to promote political or social causes.
  • Nation-state attackers – Highly funded groups targeting governments or infrastructure.

Understanding who might attack a system helps organizations choose the right defenses.

Why Understanding Threats Matters

Recognizing different types of threats allows users and organizations to:

  • Choose appropriate security controls
  • Respond faster to incidents
  • Reduce damage from attacks
  • Protect sensitive data

Cybersecurity Careers & How People Get Into Them

Cybersecurity is a broad field with many different roles. Most professionals do not start directly in cybersecurity.

A common path is: IT or Networking → Cybersecurity specialization.

Employers care about three things: skills, certifications, and experience.

Core Cybersecurity Roles

🔐 Security Analyst

What they do:

Security Analysts monitor systems for threats, investigate alerts, analyze logs, and respond to suspicious activity. This is one of the most common entry‑level cybersecurity roles.

Typical background:

  • IT support or help desk
  • Networking fundamentals
  • Basic scripting or log analysis

Certifications explained:

  • Security+ – Proves understanding of core security concepts
  • CySA+ – Focuses on detection, monitoring, and analysis

🧱 Security Engineer

What they do:

Security Engineers design and maintain secure systems. They configure firewalls, access controls, monitoring tools, and network security devices.

Typical background:

  • Networking or systems administration
  • Windows/Linux administration
  • Understanding of firewalls and network traffic

Certifications explained:

  • Network+ – Networking fundamentals (very important)
  • Security+ – Security concepts on top of networking
  • CCNA – Practical networking and infrastructure skills

🕵️ Penetration Tester (Ethical Hacker)

What they do:

Penetration Testers legally attempt to break into systems to find vulnerabilities before attackers do. This is not an entry‑level role.

Typical background:

  • Strong networking knowledge
  • Linux and command‑line experience
  • Scripting (Python, Bash)

Certifications explained:

  • CEH – Introductory ethical hacking concepts
  • PNPT – Real‑world penetration testing methodology
  • OSCP – Advanced, hands‑on hacking certification

🚨 Incident Responder

What they do:

Incident Responders handle active cyber incidents such as ransomware, breaches, or compromised accounts. They contain damage and restore systems.

Typical background:

  • Security analyst or SOC experience
  • Understanding of malware and attack techniques
  • Strong documentation and communication skills

Certifications explained:

  • Security+ – Foundation knowledge
  • GCIH – Incident handling and response

📄 Governance, Risk & Compliance (GRC)

What they do:

GRC professionals focus on policies, risk management, audits, and compliance with laws and standards. This role is less technical but very important.

Typical background:

  • IT knowledge
  • Strong communication and documentation skills
  • Understanding of regulations and policies

Certifications explained:

  • Security+ – General security understanding
  • CISM – Security management
  • CISSP – Senior‑level certification

Education & Career Path Summary

  • Start with IT & networking fundamentals
  • Earn entry‑level certifications
  • Gain hands‑on experience
  • Specialize into a cybersecurity role

There is no shortcut. Cybersecurity professionals are trusted because they understand how systems actually work.

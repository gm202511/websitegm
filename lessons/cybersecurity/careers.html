Cybersecurity Careers & How to Get Started

Cybersecurity is not a single job. It is a field made up of many roles that focus on protecting systems, investigating incidents, designing secure networks, and managing risk.

There is no single path. Many cybersecurity professionals start in IT, networking, help desk, or programming roles before specializing.

Common Cybersecurity Roles

🔐 Security Analyst

What they do: Monitor systems, review alerts, investigate suspicious activity.

Good for: Problem solvers who like investigation and analysis.

Certifications: Security+, CySA+

🧱 Security Engineer

What they do: Build and maintain secure systems and networks.

Good for: Strong technical thinkers.

Certifications: Network+, Security+, CCNA

🕵️ Penetration Tester (Ethical Hacker)

What they do: Test systems to find weaknesses before attackers do.

Good for: Curious, hands‑on learners.

Certifications: CEH, PNPT, OSCP (advanced)

🚨 Incident Responder

What they do: Respond to breaches, contain damage, restore systems.

Good for: Calm thinkers under pressure.

Certifications: Security+, GCIH

📄 GRC (Governance, Risk, Compliance)

What they do: Policies, audits, compliance, risk management.

Good for: Students strong in communication and organization.

Certifications: Security+, CISM, CISSP

Education & Entry Paths

  • High School: IT, networking, cybersecurity fundamentals
  • College: Cybersecurity, IT, Computer Science
  • No‑Degree Path: Certifications + labs + real experience

Many employers care more about skills, certifications, and experience than the specific school you attended.

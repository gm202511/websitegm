Cybersecurity Careers & How to Get Started

Cybersecurity is not one job — it is a field made up of many different roles. Some focus on defense, others on investigation, design, or policy.

There is no single “right” path. Many professionals start in IT, networking, programming, or help desk roles before moving into cybersecurity.

Common Cybersecurity Roles

🔐 Security Analyst

What they do: Monitor systems, review security alerts, investigate suspicious activity.

Good for: People who like problem‑solving and investigation.

Certifications: Security+, CySA+

🧱 Security Engineer

What they do: Design, build, and maintain secure systems and networks.

Good for: Strong technical thinkers who enjoy building systems.

Certifications: Network+, Security+, CCNA

🕵️ Penetration Tester (Ethical Hacker)

What they do: Test systems to find vulnerabilities before attackers do.

Good for: Curious, technical problem‑solvers.

Certifications: CEH, PNPT, OSCP (advanced)

🚨 Incident Responder

What they do: Respond to breaches, contain damage, and help systems recover.

Good for: Calm thinkers who work well under pressure.

Certifications: Security+, GCIH (later career)

📄 GRC (Governance, Risk & Compliance)

What they do: Create policies, ensure compliance, manage risk.

Good for: Students who like policy, communication, and structure.

Certifications: Security+, CISM, CISSP (advanced)

Education Paths

  • High School: IT, networking, cybersecurity fundamentals
  • College: IT, Computer Science, Cybersecurity degrees
  • No‑Degree Path: Certifications + labs + experience

Many employers care more about skills, certifications, and experience than where you went to school.

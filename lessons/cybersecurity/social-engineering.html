Social Engineering

Social engineering is a type of cyber attack that tricks people into giving away information or access instead of hacking a system directly.

Attackers take advantage of trust, fear, urgency, curiosity, or authority to manipulate users into making mistakes.

Common Social Engineering Attacks

  • Phishing: Fake emails or messages trying to steal information
  • Pretexting: Making up a story to convince you to share data
  • Baiting: Offering something tempting (free downloads, USB drives)
  • Tailgating: Physically following someone into a secure area
  • Impersonation: Pretending to be IT, a boss, or a trusted company
